# EcoSphere — Carbon Footprint Calculation Methodology

This document outlines the mathematical equations, emission factors, and scientific baselines utilized by the EcoSphere calculation engine (`lib/calculations.ts`) to estimate personal greenhouse gas footprints. All emission factors are derived from standard environmental databases (such as the UK DEFRA Greenhouse Gas Reporting Factors and the US EPA Emissions & Generation Resource Integrated Database).

---

## 📐 General Equation

A user's total annual carbon footprint (expressed in tonnes of carbon dioxide equivalent, $\text{t CO}_2\text{e}$) is the sum of four resource categories:

$$\text{Total Footprint} = \text{Home Energy} + \text{Transportation} + \text{Diet \& Waste} + \text{Shopping}$$

Each component is calculated annually and divided by $1,000$ to convert kilograms ($\text{kg}$) to metric tonnes ($\text{t}$).

---

## 1. Home Energy & Utilities

Estimates emissions generated by residential power, heating, and water consumption.

### Calculation Formula:
$$\text{Home Energy (t)} = \frac{(E_{\text{monthly}} \times 12 \times EF_e) + (G_{\text{monthly}} \times 12 \times EF_g) + (W_{\text{monthly}} \times 12 \times EF_w)}{1000}$$

Where:
- $E_{\text{monthly}}$: Average monthly electricity consumption in kilowatt-hours ($\text{kWh}$).
- $G_{\text{monthly}}$: Average monthly natural gas consumption in cubic meters ($\text{m}^3$).
- $W_{\text{monthly}}$: Average monthly clean water usage in liters ($\text{L}$).

### Emission Coefficients ($EF$):
| Resource | Factor | Unit | Reference Base |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Electricity** ($EF_e$) | `0.4` | $\text{kg CO}_2\text{e / kWh}$ | Grid average carbon intensity |
| **Natural Gas** ($EF_g$) | `2.0` | $\text{kg CO}_2\text{e / m}^3$ | Stationary combustion of natural gas |
| **Water** ($EF_w$) | `0.001` | $\text{kg CO}_2\text{e / L}$ | Wastewater treatment & supply routing |

---

## 2. Transportation & Travel

Assesses commuting footprint across personal vehicles, public transit systems, and aviation.

### Calculation Formula:
$$\text{Transportation (t)} = \frac{(D_{\text{vehicle}} \times 52 \times EF_v) + (D_{\text{transit}} \times 52 \times EF_t) + (F_{\text{short}} \times EF_{fs}) + (F_{\text{long}} \times EF_{fl})}{1000}$$

Where:
- $D_{\text{vehicle}}$: Average weekly distance driven in a personal vehicle ($\text{km}$).
- $D_{\text{transit}}$: Average weekly distance traveled via train, subway, or bus ($\text{km}$).
- $F_{\text{short}}$: Number of short-haul flights ($< 3$ hours) taken per year.
- $F_{\text{long}}$: Number of long-haul flights ($> 3$ hours) taken per year.

### Emission Coefficients ($EF$):
| Travel Mode | Factor | Unit | Details |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Petrol Vehicle** | `0.22` | $\text{kg CO}_2\text{e / km}$ | Standard internal combustion engine (ICE) |
| **Diesel Vehicle** | `0.20` | $\text{kg CO}_2\text{e / km}$ | Diesel combustion vehicle |
| **Hybrid Vehicle** | `0.12` | $\text{kg CO}_2\text{e / km}$ | Mild & Plug-in Hybrid Electric vehicles |
| **Electric Vehicle (EV)** | `0.05` | $\text{kg CO}_2\text{e / km}$ | Powered by grid-sourced battery recharge |
| **Public Transit** ($EF_t$) | `0.04` | $\text{kg CO}_2\text{e / km}$ | Average passenger rail / transit bus occupancy |
| **Short Flight** ($EF_{fs}$) | `150.0`| $\text{kg CO}_2\text{e / flight}$ | Domestic and regional flights |
| **Long Flight** ($EF_{fl}$) | `500.0`| $\text{kg CO}_2\text{e / flight}$ | Transcontinental and international flights |

---

## 3. Diet, Food, & Waste

Accounts for agricultural emissions of food consumption habits, offset by recycling routines.

### Calculation Formula:
$$\text{Diet \& Waste (t)} = \frac{\max(500, \text{Diet Factor} + \sum \text{Recycling Offsets})}{1000}$$

- The calculation uses a baseline floor clamp of **$500\text{ kg CO}_2\text{e / year}$** ($0.5\text{ T}$) to ensure a realistic biological minimum footprint for respiration and nutrition supply chains.

### Diet Coefficients:
| Diet Category | Annual Emissions | Details |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Heavy Meat Eater** | `2500 kg` | Frequent red meat (beef, lamb) consumption |
| **Average Meat Eater** | `1700 kg` | Balanced poultry, fish, and occasional red meat |
| **Vegetarian** | `1200 kg` | Meat-free, dairy and egg inclusive nutrition |
| **Vegan** | `900 kg` | 100% plant-based food consumption |

### Recycling Offsets:
Actively sorting and recycling waste streams diverts materials from landfill degradation (reducing methane generation) and reduces raw material production demands:
- **Paper / Cardboard**: $-50\text{ kg CO}_2\text{e / year}$
- **Plastics / Bottles**: $-80\text{ kg CO}_2\text{e / year}$
- **Glass / Cans**: $-40\text{ kg CO}_2\text{e / year}$
- **Compost / Food Scraps**: $-120\text{ kg CO}_2\text{e / year}$

---

## 4. Shopping & Retail Baseline

To represent secondary indirect consumer emissions (clothing manufacture, electronics assembly, services), EcoSphere applies a standardized baseline footprint:

$$\text{Shopping (t)} = 0.8\text{ t CO}_2\text{e / year}$$

This baseline accounts for default consumer overheads that apply to all individuals residing in industrialized grids.

---

## 🌲 Tree Equivalency Index

To translate abstract carbon values into tangible concepts, EcoSphere calculates the number of mature trees required to absorb a user's cumulative carbon savings:

$$\text{Tree Equivalent} = \frac{\text{Cumulative Savings (kg)}}{22}$$

- **Scientific Basis**: A mature tree absorbs approximately **$22\text{ kg}$** of carbon dioxide per year from the atmosphere.
